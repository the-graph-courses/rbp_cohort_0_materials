Instructions

Welcome!

Today’s exercise will be done in groups of 2 to 4. You are allowed to pick your own partners. Once you have found a group, you can go into any of the small pair rooms to work.

Each member in the group should follow the instructions below:

  1. First download the course repo here. Unzip the folder, then click on the “.Rproj” file. Then, from the Files pane of RStudio, open the “week_03” folder.

  2. In the “rmd” folder in there, the instructions for your exercise are outlined.

  3. Each group should pick one of the five datasets in the “week_03/data” folder. (You can read through the document titled “00_info_about_each_dataset” to get information about the datasets.)

  4. Then each member of the group should pick one categorical variable from that dataset. Your task is to create a short R-Markdown-based HTML report showing the frequency distribution of this variable across two sexes.

    For example, Jane and John pick the India TB dataset. Jane looks at the frequency distribution of the education variable for men and women. And John looks at the distribution of the employment variable between men and women. They each work on their own, but the final document for submission will be a single HTML containing a section on the education variable, and a section on the employment variable.

  5. Each section of the report should contain:

    1. A plot created with {ggplot2}/{esquisse}

    2. A table created with {flextable} (Your table should have a caption/title. See the flextable book for tips: https://ardata-fr.github.io/flextable-book/)

    3. At least one use of inline R code within the Rmd.

    4. At least one mention of a way the report could still be improved.

  6. As mentioned above, since RStudio cloud caused some problems in the last session (specifically with esquisse), it is recommended that you first work on your local RStudio, then when you are finished, you can:

    1. both copy your Rmd code into a document on RStudio Cloud, and do the final render from there;

    2. copy the material onto one of the group member’s computers, and perform the final render from there.

  7. When you are done with the final render, you should share your document with the Rpubs service. This can be done as described here.

  8. At 7:20pm UTC+2, a single person from the group will present the work done so far. Your work does not have to be finished by the end of the lesson. You’ll just present what you succeeded at doing and what you struggled with. The final due date is Tuesday, Nov 1 at 23:59pm UTC+2.

Finally, note that your work will be judged simply on whether you have the requirements spelled out above; it does not have to an amazing document.
